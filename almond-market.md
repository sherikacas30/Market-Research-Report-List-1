<p><p>The almond market is divided into two primary types: shelled and inshell. Shelled almonds are processed, removing the outer shell, making them ready for direct consumption or use in products like snacks, baked goods, and nut butters. Inshell almonds retain their outer shell, offering a longer shelf life and serving primarily as a snack option. Both types cater to different consumer preferences and market demands, with shelled almonds often commanding higher prices due to their convenience and processing.</p></p>
