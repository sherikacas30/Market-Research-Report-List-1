<p><p>Nanofibrillated Cellulose (NFC) refers to nanoscale cellulose fibers that enhance mechanical strength and functionality in various applications. The market includes three main types: Nanofibrillated Cellulose, characterized by its fine fibers and high aspect ratio; Nanocrystalline Cellulose, which consists of crystalline regions providing strength and stability; and Bacterial Nanocellulose, produced by microorganisms, known for its purity and biocompatibility. These cellulose derivatives are used in industries like packaging, pharmaceuticals, and textiles, capitalizing on their unique properties.</p></p>
