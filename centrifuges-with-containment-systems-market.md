<p><p>Centrifuges with containment systems are classified into two main types: vertical and horizontal. Vertical centrifuges operate in an upright position, optimizing space and providing efficient separation of solids and liquids, often used in laboratories and smaller-scale operations. Horizontal centrifuges, on the other hand, are designed to operate parallel to the ground, allowing for larger volumes and enhanced sedimentation, making them ideal for industrial applications. Both types are essential for ensuring safety while handling hazardous materials during separation processes.</p></p>
