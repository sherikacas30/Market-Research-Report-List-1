<p><p>Carbon nanotubes (CNTs) are cylindrical nanostructures with exceptional mechanical, electrical, and thermal properties. They are classified primarily into two types: single-walled carbon nanotubes (SWNTs) and multi-walled carbon nanotubes (MWNTs). SWNTs consist of a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a cylindrical formation, offering superior electrical conductivity. In contrast, MWNTs comprise multiple concentric layers, enhancing structural strength and thermal stability. Both types cater to diverse applications in electronics, materials science, and nanotechnology, driving significant market growth.</p></p>
