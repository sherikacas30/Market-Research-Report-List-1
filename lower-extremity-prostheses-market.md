<p><p>The lower extremity prostheses market encompasses various types designed to replace lost limbs at different levels. Above-knee prostheses are used for amputations above the knee, offering mobility and stability. Below-knee prostheses are suitable for amputations below the knee, allowing for a more natural gait. Ankle prostheses assist those with ankle loss, enhancing foot function. Hip disarticulation prostheses cater to individuals with amputations at the hip joint, providing support and facilitating movement while maximizing comfort and functionality.</p></p>
