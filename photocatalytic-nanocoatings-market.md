<p><ul><li>Water-Borne</li><li>Solvent-Borne</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The photocatalytic nanocoatings market is primarily divided into two types: water-borne and solvent-borne. Water-borne photocatalytic coatings utilize water as the primary solvent, offering eco-friendly properties and lower volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions. They are ideal for indoor applications and environments sensitive to pollutants. In contrast, solvent-borne photocatalytic coatings employ organic solvents, providing enhanced performance and adhesion on various surfaces. These coatings are often preferable for outdoor applications where durability and resistance to harsh conditions are required.</p></p>
