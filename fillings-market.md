<p><p>Fillings market types can be categorized into bakeable and no-bakeable options. Bakeable fillings are designed to withstand high temperatures and retain their flavor and texture during baking, making them ideal for pastries, cakes, and pies. No-bakeable fillings, on the other hand, are formulated for use in chilled or room-temperature desserts and require no cooking; these are often used in cheesecakes, cream pies, and desserts that rely on refrigeration to set. Both types cater to diverse culinary needs and preferences.</p></p>
