<p><p>Optical tweezers and magnetic tweezers are advanced tools used in mechanobiology to manipulate and study small biological samples at the molecular level. Optical tweezers use focused laser beams to trap and move microscopic particles, enabling precise control of biomolecules and cells. Magnetic tweezers employ magnetic fields to exert forces on magnetic beads attached to biomolecules, allowing researchers to investigate mechanical properties and interactions. Together, these technologies provide valuable insights into cellular mechanics, molecular interactions, and biological processes.</p></p>
