<p><p>The automotive damper and shock absorber market comprises primarily two types: hydraulic and pneumatic. Hydraulic dampers utilize fluid dynamics to absorb and dissipate energy from road irregularities, providing stability and comfort. They are commonly used due to their effectiveness and cost-efficiency. On the other hand, pneumatic dampers employ compressed air to adjust the suspension system's response to varying loads and road conditions, offering enhanced adaptability and ride quality, particularly in higher-end vehicles.</p></p>
