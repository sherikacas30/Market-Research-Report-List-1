<p><p>The low starch content potato market features two primary types: conventional and micropropagation. Conventional low starch potatoes are cultivated using traditional farming methods, focusing on soil and climate conditions to maintain their low starch levels. In contrast, micropropagation involves cultivating disease-free potato plants in a lab setting, ensuring uniformity and higher yields. Both types cater to health-conscious consumers and the food industry, as they are preferred for products requiring lower starch content, such as specialty fries and healthier snacks.</p></p>
